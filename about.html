About Me

I am Dr. Chandrashekhar Himmatrao Patil, an academician, researcher, and mentor with over 19 years of teaching experience and more than 5 years of academic administrative leadership in higher education. I currently serve as an Associate Professor in the School of Computer Science at MIT World Peace University, Pune, where I have also served as the Head of School.

I hold a Ph.D. in Computer Science specializing in Digital Image Processing and am recognized as an approved Ph.D. guide. I have successfully supervised 3 Ph.D. scholars (with 1 ongoing), contributing to impactful research and academic excellence. My research portfolio includes over 100+ publications, including 16 Scopus-indexed journal papers, 36+ international conference publications, 2 Scopus book chapters, and 1 book publication.

I also hold 8 patents (5 granted and 3 published) and 3 copyrights, reflecting my dedication to innovation and real-world technological advancements. My academic leadership includes key roles such as Chairman of the Board of Studies, Member of the Academic Council, IQAC, and Chair of the Entrance Examination Committee for multiple academic sessions.

I have been honored with the Ideal Teacher Award (2024) and the Best Teaching Staff Award (2015), acknowledging my commitment to student-centric, value-driven education. My core research interests include Digital Image Processing, Natural Language Processing, Medical Imaging, Machine Learning, Data Mining, Signal Processing, Computer Networks, and Algorithm Design.

With a passion for research, innovation, and academic development, I strive to foster a learning environment that promotes excellence, creativity, and technological growth aligned with global educational standards.
